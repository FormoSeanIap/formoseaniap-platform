Games and stories about time traveling never disappear in the history of creation. One of the biggest success on this topic in games should be *Steins;Gate*. Few years ago, a Korean comic named *Timing* left me a deep impression. *Erased*, a Japanese manga released more recently was one of the outstanding works as well.

Why? Why are works with the theme of time traveling so endless? What kind of attractions are here, which makes this fully utilized topic constantly reappearing and widely spread?

In my opinion, one of the main reasons should be the mystery of time. What exactly is “time”? There is never a satisfactory answer. In philosophy, many people think that it is related to causation, while others think change is itself the flow of time. In early 20th century, a philosopher even declared that time is unreal.

Another reason, which is the most important one in my opinion, is reflected in all the works mentioned above. Those who has time-traveling power keeps throwing themselves into correcting the mistakes they have made. They try desperately, again and again, again and again…

Because of regret.

Regrets make a great part in our life experience. Not only because we regret almost everything we did, but more importantly, these regrets will keep re-deducting in different ways in our later life, becoming the shadow that cannot be erased in our minds.

Have you ever flashed into a piece of the past suddenly in the late of night, peeling it layers by layers, then sinking into the endless vortex. “What if I…” or “If I could go to the past, I would have…”

Right, we never stop regretting.

Wounds will heal in the baptism of time, but the scars left will only be more unforgettable. The deep emotions find their own way in the imagination of time-traveling power.

In *Life is Strange*, the guilt and affection to the best friend led to the emotion of regret, and the main axis of the whole game.

The images and music in this game are breathtaking. Like watching a well-made movie while playing, this game is literally worth savoring every moment with your heart.

There are segments for pure watching in the beginning, ending, and some parts in every chapter. I sincerely recommend, with enough time, that just throw yourself into the whole game, to feel the satisfaction in the journey.

For the plot, the first part which amazed me the most was the ending of chapter three. It almost reboots the whole game through a long time traveling. This is one of my favorite part, but after realizing that it was actually not a real reboot, I was a little disappointed. This part could have been something more outstanding.

This part was talking primarily about a new ability found accidentally by Maxine, or simply Max. To go to the far past by just gazing at the photo with her, she can impact things in the photo, and fundamentally change the whole world right now. Literally Super Max, right?

As a result, Max kept Chloe’s father from dying in a serious car crash. However, related to the “chaos theory” mentioned before, Chloe was paralyzed because of another serious car crash. Finally, Max was confronted with the dilemma of killing Chloe on her own or not, which was requested by Chloe herself.

At this time, most players should begin to beware of the axis implied by the plot. These attempts to change the past will prove to be ineffective, finally.

That the time-traveling ability was mainly used on Chloe also implies that Chloe will eventually become the origin of the story, which is the same time that Max used her ability for the first time. The beginning, while the ending as well. It would be the best ending for a time-traveling story.

Of course we can choose to sacrifice the Arcadia Bay in the real game. Sacrifice Chloe’s mother who always treated us nicely, Chloe’s step father who proved to be working hard behind us, the girl we wanted to save from being bullied, the cute boy who was always running after us, the preppy we hated at first but got his warning and apology before his death, the girl bossy on the surface who actually envied us a lot, and those we may not be familiar with but also the real people in the same town…

To save simply a person, but she may be more important than all the other people.

It is the last and the most important choice to sacrifice Chloe or Arcadia Bay. I have to say, it is pretty hard to make a decision in every choice, which also reveals the importance of time-traveling. The ability runs through the whole game. We keep trying to change, to make a better future, only to find us the reason for the tragedy.

In fact, I saw a same plot few years ago. It was a Japanese anime called *Puella Magi Madoka Magica*.

Every rewind is a reset of the causal thread, or actually a destruction. Numerous rewinds which are pointed just for one person, will finally make the causal thread point at the same target. Numerous causal threads converges at the same place will result in something devastating, for example, the storm.

As *The Wheel of Time* said, “The Wheel weaves as the Wheel wills, and we are only the thread of the Pattern.”

That the hard choices makes people to keep rewinding for the best choice, and even result into the tragedy in the end, is what impresses us the most.

Well, it is, however, the most confusing part when I was reviewing the game. It seems like every choice is important, but there are simply two different endings. More importantly, only the last choice decides the ending. All the choices before were wasted, which is the biggest failure in this game.

In fact, it is not really a big deal even though I called it the biggest failure. On the contrary, there are still a lot of highlights. My favorite part is the “Nightmare” in the last chapter.

This chapter defines the outstanding images and music of this game, and it is ingenious as well. Max keeps re-experiencing the choices she made with her ability. The bad results based on goodwill showed players in a horrible way. Who were not shocked by Mr. Jefferson while trying to leave the empty classroom?

This part also reminded me of *Layers of Fear*, another game which is also famous for its awesome images. This game is better than *Life is Strange* in the designation of puzzles. Too many unnecessary and trivial puzzles slow down and destroy the game experience directly. Well, I still have no idea where to find the bottles.

There are still few defectives to discuss, like the nosebleeding to limit the ability but no more description, no more plots for the Prescott, and the using of a photo without Max herself to go back in the ending…etc.

But it does not really matter. After all, we can certainly say that this game is just awesome. It could be one of the most valuable game in the year.

To conclude, this game has apparent merits and demerits, but thinking about its outstanding part and price, I will give it the score of seven points in ten points.
